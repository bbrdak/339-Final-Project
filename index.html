The Boston Crusaders Drum and Bugle Corps

Welcome to my Boston Crusaders info page! Founded in 1940 as The Most Precious Blood Crusaders, the organization has had a storied history over the past 82 years. The group has competed as a member of Drum Corps International since 1972. Keep reading on this page to learn more about D.C.I. and the Boston Crusaders, or check out the navigation above to find pages on recent shows!

The Drum Corps International Logo

What is Drum Corps International?

Drum Corps International, abbreviated as D.C.I, is a competitive circuit for Drum and Bugle Corps from around the world. Often referred to as "marching music's major league", DCI plays host to the best marching music ensembles in the world. Members range in age from 14 to 21, coming from a wide variety of locations, backgrounds, and experiences.

Corps Sections

The Brass Section is made up of Trumpets, Mellophones, Baritones, Euphoniums, and Tubas. With about 80 members, it is the largest in the Corps!

The Season

Each corps spends several weeks learning and polishing their show, before embarking on a tour of the nation, performing at various D.C.I. organized competitions. The ensembles are scored the categories of Music, Visual, and General Effect. This competition season culminates at Drum Corps International Finals in Indianapolis, held in Lucas Oil stadium, where the corps put on their best performances of the year and are awarded their final placements.
